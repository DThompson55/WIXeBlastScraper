Sunday Service "America the Beautiful"

As we approach election day, Rev. Josh reflects on what is noble and good about the United States of America, and what is at stake as voters go to the polls. No matter the outcome on November 5th, how do we support each other in the aftermath? How do we, together, remain true to the American vision peace, liberty and justice for all? 

Coordinator: Rev. Josh Pawelek.

Children & Youth Ministry News


CYM Needs Your Help!

Do you have a spiritual practice that you engage in? It could be daily, annually, or anywhere in between. Would you be willing to visit our Elementary classroom once this upcoming program year to simply tell or show them about your spiritual practice and why you do it? Need not be more than 5-10 minutes but can take as long as you'd like. If yes, please email Emmy Galbraith, the Director of Children & Youth Ministry at dcym@uuse.org.

Care Packages


﻿Sunday, September 29th through Sunday, November 3rd: The High School Youth Group will be collecting items for Care Packages to send to our young adults beginning the post-high school chapter of their lives. Please consider donating protein bars, soup, tea, candy, snacks, toiletries, stationary, fun items - anything that a college student or young adult just starting out might enjoy. Not sure what to buy? Click here to check out our Care Package Amazon Wish List. Or, consider making a monetary donation to help defray mailing costs. Look for the bins in the lobby and on the Garden Level for the collection location. We also welcome notes of encouragement to this year's recipients. Contact Michelle Spadaccini at michspad@comcast or 860-543-0490 for more information. Thanks for your support!

Coffeehouse and Open Mic

Saturday, November 2 at 6:30 P.M.

The Coffeehouse and Open Mic is held monthly on the first Saturdays October through May. Musicians, Poets and Storytellers share their talents. Heart Centered Kirtan

Monday, November 18, 2024 at 5:30 P.M.

Clear your mind and soothe your soul! Since 2013, Heart Centered Kirtan has been traveling throughout New England with their original call-and-response devotional music. HCK weaves Sanskrit and Buddhist mantras into a unique blend of modern Western musical styles, creating a meditative and uplifting experience. All are welcome including kids of all ages. $15 suggested donation.

Love Your Neighbor Dinner

Monday, November 18, 2024 at 5:30 P.M.

The Connecticut Council of Interreligious Understanding (CCIU) Love Your Neighbor Dinner will be held at the Hartford Marriott at 200 Columbus Boulevard in Hartford on November 18. Deadline to sign up is Friday, November 8th. Rev. Dr. Alvan N. Johnson, CCIU Co-Chair is the event emcee. Frank Harris III, former columnist at the Hartford Courant and Chair of the Department of Journalism at Southern Connecticut State University is the keynote speaker. Tickets are $125 per person or $800 for a table of eight. Look for the flyer in the UUSE lobby and around the building.

Fall Clean-Up

Here's a great congregational event to build community and prepare the grounds for our guests for the holiday fair! Join the Building & Grounds Committee for the annual Fall Clean-up on Saturday, November 16th, 9 A.M. - 12 noon. Bring what you have for raking and pruning and we'll supply the rest: snacks, coffee, cider, equipment, guidance and a few laughs. Children and youth with responsible adults are especially invited. Can't make it November 16th? We'll be doing some work the week before. Contact Cory Clark or Jane Osborn for more information.

GHIAA Power Summit
